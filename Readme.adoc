Guide and notes for an unofficial https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Key_signing_party[key signing party] at https://seagl.org[SeaGL] https://pretalx.seagl.org/2025/[2025].

== üìú Guide

. Follow and refer to
.. https://wiki.debian.org/Keysigning[the Debian keysigning guide].
.. https://seagl.org/coc[the SeaGL code of conduct].
. Create your keypair if you don't already have one.
. Share your public key publicly.
. Add a row to the table below to share your key ID, proof, and signed key sharing preference.
. Bring to the party:
.. OPTIONAL: some form of government-issued ID (passport or similar). Maybe better: include a link to your proof in the table below.
.. Printouts with your name, email, key ID and algorithm, key fingerprint.
. After the party / in private:
.. Sign keys.
.. Distribute keys according to key owners' preferences.

== üìë Notes

This table exists primarily to track **key IDs** and **key owner signed key sharing preference**.
That's it.
Name and email are optional.

|===
|Name (optional) |Email user (optional) |Email domain (optional) |Key ID |Proof |Signed key sharing preference

|Adam Monsen |haircut |gmail.com |`0xA9A14F22F57DA182` |https://adammonsen.com/seagl-2025-keysigning-party-proof/[adammonsen.com] |upload to `keyserver.ubuntu.com`
| |root |punkto.org |`0xF28844F4C151E433` |https://punkto.org[punkto.org] |send me the file somehow
|Ted Matsumura |matsumura |gmail.com |`0x83995F6352AD05FA` |will bring Govt ID to show |upload to `keyserver.ubuntu.com`
|===

**Email** address is split into two columns as a weak anti-spam obfuscation.

**Key ID** is the last 16 hex characters of your primary/main/Certify key.
You can get this by running `gpg --keyid-format 0xlong -k youremail@example.com` and looking at the first line.

**Proof** is a signed attestation like https://adammonsen.com/seagl-2025-keysigning-party-proof/[this].

**Signed key sharing preference** tells folks what to do with your key after they sign it.
